First Day of 6th Grade

I might have trouble speaking about this because Coronavirus kind of ruined my first day; but here goes: I was all alone; surrounded by lip curling classmates and blood thirsty teachers. Just kidding! Ha ha! But I can kind of relate (serious face: on).


Image by Free-Photos from Pixabay

Teachers

Since I chose online school, I barely knew anyone there. I saw which class I was assigned to and I freaked out because the teacher’s profile picture had a mad drawing on it. That really taught me a lesson as today she’s one of my best teachers and I should never ever judge a book by its cover. Oh, and as for the story behind the angry drawing? She was (and still is!) so nice that she put up one of her students’ drawings. My second teacher another one of my best teachers, because she was really kind to me and she always appreciated my work; and everything I did.


Image by Jess Bailey from Pixabay

Classmates

Now about my classmates. At first I was extremely worried. I had a terrible feeling that there were going to be bullies, and there were. Whenever they made a mean comment about me, I just laughed along with them. Sometimes, I even made a sarcastic but funny comment back. In the end, all those bullies became my friends (but not like, best friends) and we would just help out each other.

Note: if you have a bully that just won’t stop, you can either just ignore them or report them to your teacher. No! You are not being a tattletale if you do this! This is so very important to know, and I wish everyone knew this.

If someone you know is hurting you mentally and especially physically you should go and talk to someone with control for example a teacher, a parent, etc! If the bully has physically hurt you the best thing to do is to talk to your parents, and then if it gets worse you can either just self defend yourself (if you are trained to do so) or address the police.


Image by S. Hermann & F. Richter from Pixabay

Friends

There were so many people in my class that I was unfamiliar with. Out of the 16 people in my class 3 went to my school, and one was my friend. My BFFs from grade 5 though, were not there.

When I first came into the class call, I was sure that there were going to be at least a few people from my previous year class, maybe even my friends! Welp, I was wrong about that one (Guess the world isn't so small, after all )

But, if I actually think back to it, I was pretty lucky! I have so many more friends, and therefore I have become so much more social! If you really think about it; when people have those few close friends, they tend to stick mostly around them, and kind of zone out from other possible friendships.


Image by huoadg5888 from Pixabay

To sum it all up:

Granted from what I put above, my first day made me feel a mix of emotions; such as stress, surprise, and excitement. Put them all together, and you have the best day ever!!

Thanks for reading!!
